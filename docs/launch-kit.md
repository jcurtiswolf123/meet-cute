# Meet Cute — LinkedIn Launch Kit

Goal: drive ~1,000 applications to **https://hellomeetcute.com/apply** from a LinkedIn push by the founder, Joshua Wolf.

Ground truth (do not embellish): Meet Cute is an invite-only, human-run matchmaking service. No swiping, no algorithm feed. Anyone can apply with a light profile (name, mobile, city, what they're looking for, LinkedIn, Instagram). A real matchmaker reviews who's looking and texts two people individually — "I think you'd hit it off with X, here's a bit about them + their Instagram. Want an intro? Reply Y/N." If both say yes, they're connected in a group text with the matchmaker. Premium, high-signal, low-effort for the applicant. Currently NYC and SF.

Honesty rules baked in: no fake stats, no fake testimonials, no "thousands of members," no emoji, minimal hashtags.

## 4. FAQ

**Is it free to apply?**
Yes — applying is free. You share a short profile and the matchmaker reviews who's looking. (Whatever your live pricing is for the matchmaking service itself, state it plainly on the site; don't overstate it here.)

**How is this different from a dating app?**
There's no app to scroll, no swiping, and no public profile. A real person reviews who's looking and makes introductions one at a time. You're not browsing strangers — you're being thoughtfully introduced.

**Who sees my information?**
The matchmaker. When there's a potential introduction, you get a private text with a few details about the other person and their Instagram — and they get the same about you — only if both of you say yes do you get connected. Your profile isn't posted publicly for anyone to browse.

**Is it only in NYC and SF?**
Right now, yes — New York and San Francisco. You're welcome to apply from elsewhere, and we'll keep you in mind as we expand.

**Do I have to be on it constantly?**
No. That's the point. There's nothing to maintain and no inbox to manage. You apply once, then you only hear from us when there's a real introduction worth your time.

**What happens after I apply?**
The matchmaker reviews your profile. When there's someone you'd likely hit it off with, you'll get a private text — a few real details, their Instagram, and a simple question: want an intro? Reply yes or no.

**What if I say yes and they say no (or vice versa)?**
Then no introduction happens, and there's no awkwardness — neither of you is put on the spot publicly. The matchmaker keeps looking.

**Do I need to be looking for something serious?**
Tell us what you're actually looking for when you apply. The matchmaker uses that to make better introductions, so honesty helps you, not hurts you.

## 5. Launch playbook — first two weeks

**Operating principle:** one strong post, then work the comments and DMs. LinkedIn rewards the author who replies. The link goes in the first comment and in DMs, not buried in the post body (though it's fine in the body too — both is best for a founder post).

### Cadence

**Week 1**
- **Day 1 (Mon/Tue, ~9am local):** Post variant (a), the founder story. Highest-effort, highest-reach post. Pin the apply link as the first comment.
- **Day 1, all day:** Reply to every comment within the first 2–3 hours (this drives reach). DM warm leads who engage — people you know are single or are great connectors.
- **Day 3:** Post variant (c), the "I'm personally matchmaking / tag someone" community post. This is your referral engine — explicitly ask people to tag a single friend.
- **Day 5:** Post variant (b), the short announcement, framed as a quick update ("Day 5 — the introductions have started. Still looking for great people in NYC and SF.") Keep it honest; only say what's true.
- **Throughout:** DM 5–10 warm connectors per day (see below). Personal, short, no copy-paste feel.

**Week 2**
- **Day 8:** Reshare/quote your Day 1 post with one honest new line ("A week in — here's what I'm learning about why people are done with apps."). No invented numbers.
- **Day 10:** A value post, not a pitch — e.g., 3 honest reasons dating apps fail busy people. End with a soft CTA and the apply link in the comment.
- **Day 12:** Second "tag someone" community post, targeting the opposite city or a specific group (founders, finance, creative, etc.).
- **Day 14:** Short recap + open invitation. Thank people who reshared by name.

### Per-post motion
1. **Post** (link in body for founder posts; otherwise top of body or first comment).
2. **Comment** immediately with the apply link + one line: "Apply or pass it to someone great: https://hellomeetcute.com/apply"
3. **DM warm leads** who liked/commented — short, specific, human.
4. **Tag connectors** and ask 3–5 trusted people to reshare.

### Who to tag / ask to reshare
- Friends who are well-connected social hubs (the people everyone asks "do you know anyone?").
- Founders, operators, and creatives in NYC and SF with single networks.
- Anyone who's personally told you they're frustrated with apps — they'll resonate and reshare.
- 3–5 people you'll DM directly with: "Would mean a lot if you reshared this to your NYC/SF friends — looking for great single people."
